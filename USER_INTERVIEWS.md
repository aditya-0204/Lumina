# USER_INTERVIEWS.md - Research & Validation

## Interview 1: Alex, CTO at Series A Fintech (~$8M ARR, 22 people)

**Date:** May 7, 2026  
**Duration:** 12 minutes

**Direct quotes:**
- "Honestly? I have no idea if we're overspending."
- "If someone told me I was wasting $500 a month and how to fix it, I'd do it immediately."

**Most surprising point:**
- AI tool spend was not tracked as its own budget line item

**What it changed in the product:**
- lead with a clear savings number
- make plan alternatives explicit
- explain recommendations in plain language

## Interview 2: Jordan, VP Engineering at Series B AI Startup (~$30M ARR, 45 people)

**Date:** May 7, 2026  
**Duration:** 15 minutes

**Direct quotes:**
- "Default was always buy the best plan, and it stuck."
- "Benchmarking is huge for us."
- "I’d love a tool that tells me what mix our team should actually use."

**Most surprising point:**
- A previous plan mistake had already cost them thousands per month

**What it changed in the product:**
- compare plan fit more explicitly
- keep benchmark mode on the roadmap
- surface team-size-aware recommendations

---

## Interview 3: Sam, Engineering Lead at Series A SaaS (~$4M ARR, 12 people)

**Date:** May 7, 2026  
**Duration:** 10 minutes

**Direct quotes:**
- "The moment I saw free audit, I clicked."
- "We'd definitely share this with our team."
- "I'd want to know if the recommendation is biased."

**Most surprising point:**
- Trust and transparency mattered more than visual polish

**What it changed in the product:**
- keep the output easy to share
- be explicit about how the business behind the tool makes money
- make the recommendations feel practical rather than sales-driven

---

## Cross-Interview Themes

1. AI spend is usually messy and poorly tracked
2. Users want direct, trusted recommendations
3. Shared results are valuable when the findings are surprising
4. Trust matters more than design polish on its own
