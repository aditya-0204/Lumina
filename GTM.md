# GTM Strategy - SpendAudit

## Target User

**Job title:** Engineering Manager or VP Engineering  
**Company stage:** Series A-C ($5M-$100M ARR), 15-80 person team  
**Pain point:** "We're paying for too many AI tools and I have no idea if we're overspending"  
**Trigger:** Team members requesting new AI tool access, monthly billing shock, or annual budget planning

- Google: "AI tool spending optimization", "reduce chatgpt costs", "claude vs copilot pricing"
- Product Hunt: AI tool comparison threads
- Hacker News: ML/AI launches, cost optimization posts
- Twitter: Follows @natfriedman (GitHub), @amasad (Replit), @jacksonfall (AI founders)

## First 100 Users in 30 Days ($0 paid budget)

**Week 1-2: Cold outreach + community seeding**
- Identify 50 engineering managers on Twitter with 1k-10k followers, mention them with: "Built a free audit tool - would you try it if I paid for lunch?" (personal approach, not bot-like)
- Post in 5 Slack communities' #launches or #tools channels with demo link
- Post on Product Hunt with launch day threads
- Share on Hacker News "Show HN: Free AI Spend Audit Tool" (genuine project, not marketing)

**Week 3: Organic virality loop**
- Email first 30 users asking for 1 thing: "What was the #1 thing you learned about your spending?" (Social proof factory)
- Retweet results, screenshot audits (anonymized) showing >$500/mo savings
- Reply to cost-optimization threads on Twitter/Reddit with "built something that might help, no promotion"

**Week 4: Warm network**
- Email Y Combinator batch mates / college network: "I built this, trying to get early feedback"
- Ask early users for referrals: "Know another eng manager at a startup? Refer them, I'll send you $50 in Credex credits"

**Expected outcomes:**
- Week 1: 15-20 signups (friends + direct outreach)
- Week 2: 30-40 signups (Slack + community buzz)
- Week 3: 25-35 signups (shared results, virality)
- Week 4: 20-30 signups (referrals + network)
- **Total: ~90-125 signups**
